Our story

Built where ideas, agencies, and accredited science meet.

Over years of working across hospitals, academic institutions, CROs, and regulated environments, our founders saw a recurring problem: research ideas stalled because of fragmented execution, unclear ownership, and a lack of integrated scientific oversight.

Academic research was rich in ideas but disconnected from product pathways. Industry programs were pressured by timelines and compliance but lacked early scientific continuity. CRO engagements were often transactional — focused on execution rather than on the scientific intent behind the work.

BioVeritas was established to bridge these gaps. We operate as an independent CRO platform designed to support research from concept and feasibility through non-clinical development, clinical research support, and regulatory readiness — under one quality framework, with one scientific voice.

Our approach is built on partnership, not transactions: combining scientific rigor, regulatory discipline, and practical execution so that research programs remain coherent, compliant, and purpose-driven from start to finish.

Scientific USPs

Research requires more than processes — it requires scientific judgement.

When studies encounter unexpected results, we analyse implications rather than just reporting outcomes. When regulatory pathways evolve, we adapt research strategies — together with sponsors.

01 · Integrity
Scientific judgement, not just process. Experienced scientists, clinicians, and regulatory professionals remain actively involved across the research lifecycle — from design and protocol development through data review and interpretation.
02 · Transparency
Disciplined execution and clear accountability. Structured oversight across study design, execution coordination, data review, and reporting — aligned with OECD, GCP, GLP, and applicable regulatory expectations.
03 · Experience
A consultant network of medical professionals, toxicologists, analytical specialists, and regulatory experts drawn from direct participation in study execution, data evaluation, and submission-focused work across therapeutic areas.
04 · Integration
One CRO interface across non-clinical, analytical, and clinical work. Unified scientific and quality framework, consistent oversight, and clear accountability — reducing fragmentation across the development lifecycle.
05 · Adaptability
Flexibility and timely responsiveness when programs encounter unexpected results or shifting development priorities. Engagement spans full programs or targeted involvement at critical milestones.
