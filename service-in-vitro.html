In vitro work is where a program’s scientific story begins — establishing activity, selectivity, and early safety signals that shape every downstream decision.

We design assays around the question you actually need answered, run them with appropriate reference controls, and interpret the result in the context of your development pathway.

Fit-for-purpose from the first plate

Assays are qualified against reference standards and positive/negative controls so results are defensible, reproducible, and ready to carry into IND-enabling work.

What we deliver

Our in vitro portfolio spans target engagement through early safety and mechanism, individually or as a screening cascade.

Receptor binding
Radioligand and non-radioactive binding assays for affinity, selectivity, and competition profiling.
Cytotoxicity & viability
MTT, resazurin, ATP, and LDH assays across cell lines and primary cells with dose–response modelling.
Mechanism of action
Reporter-gene, pathway, and functional assays to characterise how a candidate acts.
2D & 3D cell models
Monolayer, spheroid, and co-culture systems for more physiologically relevant readouts.
Enzyme & biochemical
Inhibition/activation kinetics and selectivity panels for target and off-target profiling.

How an engagement runs

A consistent five-stage flow keeps every engagement aligned with regulatory intent — from feasibility through a decision-ready report.

01 · Scoping

Feasibility & gap analysis

Map existing data to your target submission; identify gaps, endpoints, and study-design options.

02 · Design

Protocol & plan

Draft an OECD/ICH-aligned protocol with a statistical/analysis plan and clear acceptance criteria.

03 · Execution

Conduct

Run the work under quality oversight at accredited partner facilities with contemporaneous data capture.

04 · Analysis

Review & interpret

QA-cleared data, biostatistics where relevant, and scientific interpretation — not just raw delivery.

05 · Reporting

Decision-ready report

A structured report and data package prepared with audit and agency review in mind.

Quality & data integrity

Work is conducted under quality-managed SOPs with ALCOA+ data-integrity principles. Raw data is captured contemporaneously, archived, and made available for sponsor and agency audit.

  • ISO 9001 quality-management system across the lab's operations.
  • Independent review of protocols, conduct, and final reports before release.
  • Traceable records — every result traces back to an explicit method and standard.
  • Confidential by default — mutual NDA available within 24 hours of request.
