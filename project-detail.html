IND-enabling safety package for an oncology kinase inhibitor.

A clinical-stage US biotech needed a complete IND-enabling pre-clinical safety package delivered before their pre-IND meeting. We ran six concurrent studies in 14 weeks and met every milestone.

The sponsor's lead asset — a first-in-class kinase inhibitor for a haematologic malignancy — needed an FDA-ready safety package in less than four months to preserve their funding milestones. Prior CRO discussions had estimated 24+ weeks.

The challenge

The program had three constraints stacked against it:

  • Tight timeline. A pre-IND meeting was already scheduled in 16 weeks.
  • Limited material. Only ~80g of GMP-grade test article was available.
  • Novel target. No prior off-target liability profile existed, so safety pharmacology design had to be conservative.

Our approach

Rather than running studies sequentially, we ran six in parallel after locking the protocol package upfront. A dedicated scientific lead reviewed in-life observations daily and escalated emerging findings within 24 hours, so the sponsor could brief their FDA contacts in real time.

Why parallel execution worked here

Because the genotox battery, safety pharmacology, and 28-day repeat-dose studies depended on the same dose-range-finding output, we ran the DRF first, then triggered the rest the same week. Cross-functional QA review caught one analytical drift before it reached the GLP study.

The package comprised six GLP-aligned studies plus a non-GLP dose-range-finding study.

Outcome

The full package was delivered at week 14 — two weeks ahead of the pre-IND meeting. The sponsor used the data to refine their starting dose justification, presented a successful pre-IND briefing, and submitted the IND seven weeks later. The IND was accepted with no clinical hold within the 30-day review window.

The 28-day rat study identified a reversible reticulocyte effect that informed the Phase I monitoring plan. Because we flagged it within four days of necropsy rather than at final report, the sponsor was able to update their investigator brochure draft in parallel.

What we learned

Three operating principles got re-validated:

  1. Lock the protocol package before any study starts. Cross-study design decisions become much harder mid-flight.
  2. Run a non-GLP DRF before the GLP work. A two-week DRF saves six weeks of GLP-study redesign if dose levels were wrong.
  3. Daily scientific review beats weekly status calls. Real-time pathology + chemistry review catches signals before they become surprises.
